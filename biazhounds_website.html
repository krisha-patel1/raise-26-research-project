Our Framework

Four Original Metrics

Cognitive Bias Detection

Identified 8 types of psychological biases in headlines — mental shortcuts that distort how readers understand AI.

Power Shift Index (PSI)

Measures who's framed as in control: humans or AI. A score above 100 means AI framing exceeds human framing.

AI Anxiety Index (AAI)

Quantifies how much fear and worry headlines generate, using negative emotion words, risk language, and job threat signals.

The Invisible Human

Tracks deep human experiences — love, grief, spirituality, community — that AI coverage systematically ignores.

Evidence-Based Recommendations

What Should Change

Grounded in the data, our recommendations target the specific mechanisms creating distorted AI coverage.

For Media Organizations

  • Create editorial guidelines on cognitive bias awareness
  • Train reporters to recognize fear/optimism triggers
  • Expand coverage beyond work & efficiency narratives
  • Cover ethics, relationships, meaning-making, community
  • Develop "bias auditing" tools for headline review

For Educators

  • Teach students to identify cognitive biases in headlines
  • Include media literacy in all AI education programs
  • Use tools like our Credibility Score (0–100) for curriculum

For Readers

  • Don't rely on a single news source for AI information
  • Seek outlets with credibility scores above 50
  • Compare how different sources frame the same AI story
  • Notice when fear or optimism language appears

Media Literacy Matters: Being aware of bias doesn't mean avoiding news. It means reading critically, seeking diverse perspectives, and understanding how emotional language shapes your thinking. Our Credibility Score (0–100, where Score = 100 − Bias Intensity × 100) ranged from 35 to 65 across sources in our dataset — significant variation showing that outlet choice matters.
